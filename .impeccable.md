## Design Context

### Users
Civic researchers, journalists, policy staff, and engaged residents who need to scan city budget patterns quickly before returning to official budget documents for line-item verification.

### Brand Personality
Clear, civic, composed. The interface should feel trustworthy and contemporary without looking like a finance dashboard or a campaign site.

### Aesthetic Direction
Elegant modern civic editorial: warm paper, disciplined ink typography, precise grid lines, restrained color accents, and dense but legible data cards. Avoid decorative gradients, glassy surfaces, oversized rounded cards, and generic SaaS dashboard styling.

### Design Principles
1. Make budget comparisons easy to scan before adding visual flourish.
2. Use refined typography, thin rules, and deliberate spacing to communicate trust.
3. Keep color functional: city/category colors should carry data, while the shell stays restrained.
4. Prefer flat, well-structured surfaces over stacked cards and heavy shadows.
5. Preserve responsive readability for long labels, currency values, and chart text.
